The Dormand-Prince 8(7) method lacks the FSAL property of its 5(4) sibling, 
which is the price one pays for higher accuracy. The pioneers, Dormand and Prince,
made rational approximations to the coefficients, accurate to about 20 decimal
digits, which are reproduced in table \ref{tab:butcherdopri87}, which, for
readability, is located in an appendix.
